## Working Name: **"MirrorMe"** (or similar — we can iterate)

### Core Idea

A platform where users take curated self-evaluation assessments and receive a rich, personalized analysis of their strengths, weaknesses, behavioral tendencies, and growth areas — presented in a way that's actually *useful* and not just a generic PDF dump.

---

### Assessment Framework — What to Include

There's a spectrum here from well-established psychometrics to more modern/custom evaluations. Here's how I'd think about organizing them:

**Personality & Behavioral Style**
- **DISC** — great for communication style, workplace behavior, how you handle conflict. Well-known in corporate settings.
- **Big Five (OCEAN)** — the most scientifically validated personality model. Measures openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism. More rigorous than MBTI.
- **Enneagram** — popular for personal growth communities, maps motivation and fear patterns.
- MBTI is hugely popular but scientifically weaker — worth considering for market appeal even if we caveat it.

**Strengths-Focused**
- **CliftonStrengths (Gallup)** — identifies top talent themes. Licensing is expensive though, so we may want to build an *inspired-by* version rather than using the official one (legal consideration).
- **VIA Character Strengths** — free, well-researched, maps 24 character strengths. Great alternative to Gallup that we can actually integrate.

**Risk, Decision-Making & Cognitive Style**
- **Risk tolerance / "Risk-o-meter"** — could be custom-built. Useful for entrepreneurs, investors, career changers. Questions around comfort with ambiguity, loss aversion, decision speed.
- **Cognitive bias assessment** — how susceptible someone is to anchoring, confirmation bias, sunk cost fallacy, etc.
- **Decision-making style** — analytical vs. intuitive vs. dependent vs. avoidant.

**Emotional & Relational**
- **Emotional Intelligence (EQ)** — self-awareness, empathy, self-regulation, social skills.
- **Attachment style** — useful for personal relationships context.
- **Conflict resolution style** — Thomas-Kilmann model (competing, collaborating, compromising, avoiding, accommodating).

**Career & Purpose**
- **Holland Codes (RIASEC)** — career interest mapping.
- **Values assessment** — what someone actually prioritizes (security vs. freedom vs. impact vs. wealth, etc.).
- **Burnout risk assessment** — increasingly relevant.

### Key Design Decision: Build vs. License

For well-known assessments like DISC and CliftonStrengths, we have three paths:

1. **License the official tool** — expensive, but carries brand trust. Gallup charges $25-60 per assessment.
2. **Build "inspired by" versions** — use the same underlying constructs (which are often based on public research) but with our own questions and branding. Legally fine if done carefully.
3. **Use open/free frameworks** — Big Five, VIA Strengths, Holland Codes, and Enneagram are all in the public domain or freely available.

My instinct: start with open/free frameworks and custom-built assessments, then add licensed tools later as premium tiers.

### Target Audience — Three Strong Candidates

**Option A: Career-Oriented Professionals (25-40)**
- People in transition — considering a job change, promotion, or career pivot.
- They want clarity on what they're good at and what environments suit them.
- Monetization: B2C subscriptions, plus B2B sales to HR departments and coaching firms.
- *This is probably the biggest market and most natural fit.*

**Option B: Founders & Entrepreneurs**
- Self-awareness is a superpower for founders — understanding your risk profile, leadership blind spots, decision-making patterns.
- Smaller market but higher willingness to pay and strong community dynamics.
- Could tie into accelerator partnerships.

**Option C: Personal Growth / Self-Improvement Seekers**
- Broader audience, overlaps with therapy/coaching/wellness space.
- Driven by curiosity ("understand yourself better").
- Risk: this audience can be harder to monetize — lots of free content competing.

**Option D: Students & Early Career (18-25)**
- Figuring out what to do with their lives, what majors or career paths fit them.
- High volume, lower willingness to pay — but possible institutional sales (universities, career centers).

My take: **Option A as the primary audience** with elements of B baked in. It gives you a clear value proposition ("make better career decisions by understanding yourself") and opens both B2C and B2B revenue paths. You can expand to C and D later.

### What Makes This Different from Existing Tools

There are plenty of personality quizzes online. The differentiation needs to come from:

**1. Unified Profile Across Multiple Assessments**
Most tools give you one test result in isolation. MirrorMe would synthesize *across* tests — "Your DISC profile says you're high-D, your EQ assessment shows lower empathy scores, and your conflict style is competing. Here's what that pattern means in practice and where it creates friction."

**2. Contextual Analysis, Not Just Labels**
Instead of "You're an ESTJ," the output would address real scenarios: "In team settings, you likely… When under stress, watch out for… In negotiations, your natural tendency is…"

**3. Growth Roadmap**
After the analysis, give actionable next steps. Not just "here's who you are" but "here's what to work on and how." Could link to resources, exercises, coaching, or courses.

**4. Progress Over Time**
Let users retake assessments periodically and track how they evolve. This also drives retention.

**5. AI-Powered Insight Layer**
Use an LLM to generate personalized narrative reports, answer follow-up questions ("How does my profile affect my leadership style?"), and make cross-assessment connections that a static report can't.

### Revenue Model Ideas

- **Freemium** — one or two basic assessments free, full suite behind a paywall ($15-30/month or $99-199/year).
- **One-time deep assessment** — a comprehensive evaluation package for $49-99 that feels like a "personal diagnostic."
- **B2B / Teams** — sell to companies for team-building, hiring insight, leadership development. This is where the real money is.
- **Coaching marketplace** — connect users to certified coaches who can walk them through their results. Take a referral fee.
- **White-label** — let coaches, HR consultants, and therapists use the platform under their own brand.

### Open Questions to Resolve Next

1. **Do we want scientific rigor as a core brand pillar, or more of a "fun + insightful" vibe?** This shapes tone, assessment selection, and audience.
2. **Mobile-first or web app?** Assessments are easier on desktop but engagement/retention favors mobile.
3. **How deep is the AI layer?** Is it just generating reports, or is there an interactive "coach" experience?
4. **Competitive landscape deep-dive** — Crystal, Truity, 16Personalities, and others are already in this space. Where exactly do we wedge in?
5. **What's the MVP?** If we had to launch in 8 weeks, which 2-3 assessments and what level of analysis would we ship?
